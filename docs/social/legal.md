The Critical Missing Piece: Your framework lacks a transitional legal mechanism for initial access to privately-owned assets before they're fully socialized. This is the gap between "the capitalist owns this factory" and "the Scope Committee runs it socially." 3. Suggested Additions to Your Framework

Emergency Use Protocol

When: Assets sit idle while basic needs go unmet
Who: Affected community + nearest Scope Committee
Process:

1. Document: idle asset, unmet need, proposed social use
2. Notify: owner, relevant federation
3. Interim Use Permit (30 days, renewable):
   - No ownership transfer
   - Full accounting of use
   - Owner receives claim capacity equivalent to fair market rent
   - Use ceases if owner resumes productive use
4. During interim use:
   - Scope Committee forms among users
   - Begins transition planning (two parallel plans)
   - Observer calculates substitution ratios

This creates a legal bridge between private ownership and social use—neither trespass nor expropriation, but a metabolically-grounded claim based on actual use vs. idle speculation.

Idle Asset Protocol

Classification: Assets are "idle" if:

- Unused for > 6 months (land/factories)
- No planned use in next planning cycle
- Maintenance neglected

When idle assets exist in same region as unmet basic needs:

- Planner flags this as a solvable local contradiction
- Federation may authorize transitional use
- Owner's rights: receive fair compensation from social use, right to resume productive use with 90 days notice

You've identified a real tension. Let's think it through.

---

## The Contradiction

**Transparency requirement:** Workers need full visibility to exercise democratic control, validate labor, spot inequities, and hold committees accountable. Without this, the committee becomes a new boss and the division between mental and physical labor reasserts itself.

**Security requirement:** If fascists know exactly where critical supplies are stored, when shipments move, which factories are producing what, and who is in the committees, they can target those points for maximum disruption.

The same information that empowers workers also empowers enemies.

---

## Historical Precedent

This isn't new. Every revolutionary process faces it:

- **Paris Commune:** Published everything openly. The Versailles forces used that information to identify and execute Communards after the fall.
- **Spanish Revolution:** Anarchist collectives published detailed accounts of production. Franco's forces used captured documents to identify collectivists.
- **Chile under Allende:** Open planning and nationalizations allowed CIA and local oligarchs to target specific bottlenecks.
- **Zapatistas:** Publish extensively but keep operational details of autonomous municipalities guarded.

The pattern: **openness about what and why, closedness about when and where at fine granularity.**

---

## A Possible Resolution

The system could operate with **layered transparency**:

### Layer 1: Aggregated Public Data (Fully Open)

- What is being produced, in what quantities, at what scopes
- General flows between regions
- Socially necessary effort averages
- substitution ratios and compensation equity
- Committee decisions and meeting minutes
- Plans at coarse granularity (monthly, regional)

This builds trust, enables democratic oversight, and makes the system legible.

### Layer 2: Operational Detail (Restricted)

- Precise shipment schedules
- Exact inventory locations
- Specific transport routes and timings
- Individual worker assignments and shifts
- Fine-grained production schedules (daily, hourly)

This is what fascists would use to target.

---

## Who Sees What?

| **Role**                                | **Can See**                                                                               |
| --------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Any citizen anywhere                    | Layer 1 (aggregated public data)                                                          |
| Workers in a scope                      | Layer 1 + their own scope's Layer 2                                                       |
| Committee members                       | Layer 1 + their scope's Layer 2 + necessary inter-scope operational data for coordination |
| Federation delegates                    | Layer 1 + aggregated Layer 2 for their federation                                         |
| Social plan recipients validating labor | Only that specific labor they're validating                                               |

A worker in Mozambique can see that Luxembourg's substitution ratio is 0.9 and debate whether that's acceptable. They cannot see when the next shipment of lithium leaves the mine.

---

## The Principle

**Transparency of outcomes, opacity of operations.**

Workers need to know:

- Is the system fair? (Layer 1)
- Is my labor being validated correctly? (their own data)
- Is my committee serving us? (Layer 1)

Fascists need to know:

- Where is the vulnerable point? (Layer 2)
- When can we strike? (Layer 2)

Deny them the second while providing the first.

---

## How This Works Technically

The PlanStore already has the concept of scope ownership. Extend it:

- Each scope's detailed PlanStore is encrypted and accessible only to authenticated members of that scope
- Aggregators publish Layer 1 data derived from scopes but not revealing operational details
- Inter-scope coordination uses encrypted channels with need-to-know access
- Validation happens through blinded mechanisms—recipients confirm receipt without revealing transport details

The AT Protocol infrastructure you referenced already has capabilities for this: signed data, selective sharing, cryptographic verification without full disclosure.

---

## The Trade-off

This creates a new problem: **the committee now controls access to operational data**. That's power. That power can be abused.

The safeguard is in Layer 1 transparency about committee behavior. If a committee is suspected of hiding information from its own workers, that becomes visible in aggregated metrics—validation gaps, substitution ratio anomalies, complaints.

Workers in the scope have the right to inspect their own operational data collectively. The committee cannot hide it from them—only from outsiders. And the committee is immediately revocable.

---

## The Fascist Response to Layered Transparency

Fascists will adapt. They'll try to:

- **Infiltrate scopes** to get Layer 2 access
- **Use Layer 1 data** to infer Layer 2 (if shipments are weekly, they can watch and learn patterns)
- **Target individuals** with threats to extract information
- **Disrupt communications** to create chaos

The defense is:

- Strong scope membership authentication
- Irregular scheduling where possible
- Distributed, resilient communication
- Militia protection of critical infrastructure
- Social cohesion that makes infiltration hard

---

## The Bottom Line

The system can't be fully open about operational details without becoming vulnerable. It can't be fully closed without becoming authoritarian.

Layered transparency—**open to all about what, restricted about when and where**—is the only path through.

The Observer reports publicly on fairness, equity, and outcomes. The Planner schedules privately for security. Workers in each scope see everything about their own operation. Fascists see nothing they can strike.

# Takeover Infrastructure

_Everything a scope and its federation must have in place to survive the seizure of productive capacity. Organised by infrastructure type, then by timing: what must exist before the seizure, what must be executable on Day 0, and what must be built in the first weeks. The sequence is not optional — gaps at earlier stages collapse later stages._

---

## The Fundamental Sequence Problem

Seizure creates a race. The capitalist's counter-moves are:

1. Lock bank accounts and redirect receivables (hours to days)
2. Revoke ERP/IT access and remove records (hours)
3. Cancel supply contracts and instruct suppliers to cease delivery (days)
4. Instruct customers to redirect orders to alternative suppliers (days to weeks)
5. Initiate legal proceedings to assert property rights (days to weeks)
6. Call state intervention — police, courts, injunctions (days to weeks)
7. Call intercompany loans and assert the scope owes money to holding company (weeks)

Workers who have not pre-positioned their infrastructure will be fighting to produce with no inputs, no bank account, and no legal standing simultaneously. The capitalist's financial and legal counter-attack does not wait for workers to get organised.

**Pre-positioning is not preparation for an imagined future. It is the condition that makes seizure survivable.**

## I. Financial Infrastructure

### Pre-seizure

**Federation treasury**
The federation must maintain liquid reserves sufficient to bridge at least one seized scope for 90 days without market revenue. This is not a contingency fund — it is standing operational capacity. A scope without bridge liquidity will be forced to resume market relations on the capitalist's terms within weeks.

**Workers' financial institution**
A credit union, cooperative bank, or federation-controlled payment processor that:
- Can open accounts in the name of new legal entities within 24 hours
- Can process payroll in cash or digital workers' currency
- Can receive payments from customers redirected away from the capitalist's account
- Interoperates with conventional banking (SEPA, SWIFT, local rails) for transactions that cannot yet be internalised
- Is not subject to pressure from the capitalist's bank or the state to freeze accounts

This institution cannot be built at the moment of seizure. It must be a standing feature of the federation, built and capitalised before any individual scope moves.

**Pre-seizure account**
A federation account already opened and capitalised, designated for the seizing scope's immediate operational needs. When the capitalist's account is frozen, the federation account is active immediately — workers are paid from it on Day 1.

**Receivables surveillance**
Before seizure, the Observer must map all outstanding receivables: which customers owe money, how much, when it is due, and which bank account it will arrive in. The committee should, where possible, notify customers in advance of the intended account change — or have legal mechanisms prepared to intercept incoming payments.

### Day 0

- Federation issues emergency bridge payment covering: (a) one month payroll, (b) outstanding supplier invoices due within 30 days, (c) utility payments
- New accounts in the name of the new legal entity are activated
- Workers' payroll system is live (if ERP is locked: manual cash payroll with signed receipts)
- All incoming wire transfers are redirected: the committee contacts every known customer with the new payment details and the legal basis for the transfer

### Weeks 1–4

- VAT/tax registration in the new entity's name (immediately — continuing to collect VAT without registration creates liability)
- Insurance re-establishment for machinery, premises, liability
- New credit account opened with at least one supplier (federation guarantee may be needed as collateral)
- Accounting system established — even a spreadsheet — to track: cash position, payables, receivables, payroll, federation bridge balance

## II. Legal Infrastructure

### Pre-seizure

**Legal entity prepared in advance**
The cooperative, commune, or workers' association must be drafted and ready to register — bylaws complete, signatories designated, articles of incorporation prepared — before the seizure. Registration in many jurisdictions takes days to weeks; having the paperwork ready collapses this to hours.

The new legal entity is the counterparty for all post-seizure contracts. Without it, workers cannot sign supply agreements, open bank accounts, or assert any legal standing.

**Legal support team on standby**
Lawyers familiar with cooperative law, labour law, and property law in the relevant jurisdiction, pre-contracted by the federation, briefed on the scope and the likely capitalist counter-moves. They must be reachable within hours of the seizure.

**Pre-drafted legal responses**
- Response to injunction applications
- Response to property claims by the holding company
- Response to intercompany loan demands
- Letter to customers asserting the legal basis for redirected payments
- Letter to suppliers establishing the new counterparty relationship

These cannot be drafted under pressure while a court date is 48 hours away.

**Documentation of capitalist malfeasance**
Every deferred maintenance record, every safety violation, every financial extraction documented by the Observer during the mapping phase becomes legal material if the capitalist seeks to reassert control. Courts and public opinion both respond to evidence that the capitalist was destroying productive capacity. The Observer's reports are not only planning tools — they are legal and political records.

### Day 0

- New legal entity registered or registration filed
- Formal notice of workers' cooperative management served to: the capitalist, the relevant state authority, all known customers, all known suppliers, the tax authority
- Legal team engaged to respond to any immediate court action

### Weeks 1–4

- Transfer of regulatory licenses: environmental permits, food safety certificates, export licenses — applications filed in the new entity's name. Some will be granted readily (regulators often have discretion to maintain continuity of operation); others require political pressure
- Lease or title claim on the physical premises — the scope must assert its right to occupy; this may require legal process if the building is owned by the holding company rather than the productive entity

## III. Information Infrastructure

### Pre-seizure — the Observer's core task

**Complete scope map in a federation-controlled PlanStore**
Before seizure, the Observer must have extracted and stored, in a system the capitalist cannot lock:

- Full input inventory: all inputs, their sources, their suppliers, their prices, their contractual terms
- Full output inventory: all outputs, all customers, all contracts, all pricing, all delivery obligations
- Full equipment register: every machine, its condition, its maintenance schedule, its technical specifications, its capacity
- Full skills inventory: every worker, their skills, their certifications, their role
- Full recipe library: every production process, every technical specification, every quality standard — even if extracted informally from workers' tacit knowledge rather than formal manuals
- Full financial map: all accounts, all outstanding debts and receivables, all intercompany relationships

This is the committee's Access right exercised systematically. The goal is that when the capitalist locks the ERP system, the workers already have all the data that matters.

**Self-hosted systems**
- PlanStore: self-hosted, federation-controlled, not dependent on any capitalist cloud provider
- Observer database: same
- Worker records (payroll, hours, skills): same
- Document store (contracts, correspondence, legal records): same

The committee should mirror all obtained records to federation-controlled storage before the seizure. Once the ERP is locked, whatever has not been mirrored is gone.

**Communication infrastructure**
- A communication system (messaging, email, announcements) that the capitalist cannot shut down and the state cannot easily intercept
- Redundant channels: if the primary system is attacked, workers can coordinate through backups
- Secure channels for sensitive committee communications; open channels for worker-wide announcements

### Day 0

- ERP access revoked by capitalist: workers switch immediately to federation-controlled systems. All critical data already mirrored. Production continues from the stored recipe library and input inventory.
- Customer and supplier contact lists (extracted pre-seizure) are used immediately to make contact under the new entity's name.
- The Observer publishes the full scope map publicly — an act of transparency that simultaneously exposes the capitalist's prior conduct and legitimises the workers' management.

### Weeks 1–4

- Establish operational planning tools: a functioning PlanStore, PlanNetter, and Observer procedure running on federation infrastructure
- Onboard all workers to the new systems — turn the transition into a training exercise in economic literacy (the School of Planning in practice)

## IV. Commercial Infrastructure

### Pre-seizure

**Supplier relationships**
The committee should, during the mapping phase, make direct contact with key suppliers — not to announce the seizure in advance, but to establish that the workers know who the suppliers are, what the terms are, and that the relationship is valued. Suppliers who have met the committee are more likely to continue supply to the new legal entity.

Categorise suppliers by replaceability:
- **Critical and irreplaceable in the short run:** Prioritise maintaining these relationships through the seizure. Have the letter and the bank details ready to send on Day 0.
- **Critical but replaceable by social plan sources:** Activate Mechanism A or B (new social provider or supplier partial transition) immediately after seizure. The seizure accelerates the timeline.
- **Non-critical:** Can be renegotiated or replaced at leisure.

**Customer relationships**
Categorise customers by political susceptibility:
- **State and public bodies** (transit authorities, hospitals, schools): politically approachable. They can be asked to redirect orders and payments. They face political pressure to support workers' management. These are the first contacts on Day 0.
- **Domestic private customers:** Variable. Some will follow the relationship; others will follow legal counterparty certainty. The committee should assess which are which during the mapping phase.
- **Export customers (brands, multinationals):** Least reliable. They will redirect orders within weeks if the legal situation is unclear. The transition strategy should already be reducing dependence on these before seizure (raising `social_output_fraction`). If seizure happens at `social_output_fraction = 0.30`, the scope can lose its export customers and survive with federation support; if it happens at `social_output_fraction = 0`, it cannot.

**Pre-positioned social recipients**
Before seizure, the federation should have identified and made contact with social recipients who can absorb the scope's output from Day 1. The school federation, the transit authority, the hospital system — they must already know the scope is coming, what it produces, and how to record confirmed receipt EconomicEvents in the PlanStore. A scope that seizes with zero social recipients is immediately dependent on capitalist customers who can be instructed to redirect.

### Day 0

- Letters sent to all customers: new counterparty name, new bank details, statement of continuity of production and quality
- Orders to social recipients activated immediately — the PlanStore flow is live
- Critical supplier calls made: new counterparty, new payment account, continuation of supply requested

### Weeks 1–4

- Renegotiate all supply contracts in the new entity's name
- Identify which market customers have redirected to alternative suppliers (lost) and which have stayed (retained)
- Accelerate `social_output_fraction` to reduce dependence on any remaining market customers — the seizure is the moment to climb the ladder faster, not to consolidate at the current rung

## V. Knowledge Infrastructure

### Pre-seizure

**Recipe library**
All production processes documented in open, portable formats — not locked in proprietary ERP modules. This includes:
- Process parameters (temperatures, tolerances, speeds, times)
- Quality specifications
- Maintenance procedures
- Safety protocols
- Input specifications (what quality of input is required for each process)

Much of this exists only in workers' heads. The committee's mapping phase must convert tacit knowledge into documented recipes. This is both a planning prerequisite and a defense against the capitalist removing written records or locking technical systems.

**Skills inventory**
Which workers can operate which machines, perform which processes, supervise which functions. When management departs (and management often departs at the moment of seizure, or is expelled), the committee must know who steps into which coordination role. There must be no single points of failure — no process that only one person knows.

**Financial literacy**
Workers who take over financial management must be prepared. The committee's mapping phase is also a training programme: workers who helped document the accounts understand the accounts. The Observer procedure produces people who can run the Observer procedure.

### Day 0

- Technical manuals, process specifications, maintenance schedules: local copies on federation-controlled storage, accessible to workers
- Skills inventory consulted to assign roles: who operates which line, who coordinates which function, who manages the books

## VI. Physical and Security Infrastructure

### Pre-seizure

**Workers' militia organisation**
The seizure will face physical counter-pressure: management attempting re-entry, hired security, police enforcement of injunctions, scab recruitment. The workers' militia is not improvised at the moment of seizure — the units, the command structure, the communication protocols, and the physical defence plan are prepared in advance.

Physical defence plan for this scope specifically:
- Entry points and how they are controlled
- Communication between perimeter and interior
- Protocol for police contact (who speaks, what is said, what is not said)
- Protocol for injunction service (do not obstruct service; refer to legal team immediately)
- Media access management — transparency is defence; the camera is a weapon

**Supply of essentials for occupying workers**
If the seizure involves workers occupying the premises, they need food, water, sanitation, and communication equipment for an indefinite period. This is not an afterthought. The federation must have a supply chain for occupying scopes — the food cooperative and the solidarity network are not only social goods, they are logistics.

**Solidarity network activated**
Other scope committees in the federation must be ready to respond to a seizure with: solidarity pickets at their own workplaces, material support (food, supplies, money), and political pressure. The seizure of one scope is a test case for the federation. It must be visibly supported by the whole.

### Day 0

- Physical access controlled: management and hostile personnel cannot re-enter without committee authorisation
- Militia units at gates and key internal points
- Legal team notified and on standby for injunction response
- Media present or on-call — transparency from the first hour

## VII. Political Infrastructure

### Pre-seizure

**Legitimacy built within the scope**
The seizure must have the genuine mandate of the workforce — not a faction, not just the committee. The broadest possible worker participation in the decision, through assemblies with genuine deliberation and binding votes, is both morally required and strategically necessary: a seized scope where half the workers are uncertain is a scope where the capitalist can find allies for re-entry.

**Legitimacy built with external constituencies**
- Neighbouring communities (the scope affects them; they should be allied, not neutral)
- Other scope committees (federation solidarity)
- Social recipients (transit authorities, hospitals, schools) who depend on the scope's output
- Sympathetic press and communications capacity
- International solidarity networks (critical for export-facing scopes in low-income countries — brand reputation pressure is a real lever)

**Relationship with the state**
Assess, before seizure, what the state will do. The answer depends on:
- The political composition of the government
- The scope's strategic importance (public utilities, food production, critical infrastructure are treated differently from consumer goods)
- The international visibility of the scope
- The balance of political forces

In some conditions, the state will enforce property rights violently and immediately. In others, it will delay, negotiate, or acquiesce. The committee must have a realistic assessment, not a hopeful one. If the state will act violently within days, the seizure is only viable if the political cost of that violence is higher than the political cost of acquiescence — which depends on the solidarity network's scale and the scope's public visibility.

**Electoral and institutional pressure**
Sympathetic municipal or regional governments can: refuse police orders for eviction, provide legal cover for cooperative registration, redirect public procurement to the workers' scope, apply pressure on the state government. This is not the primary terrain of the struggle — but it is a resource, and one that should be cultivated in advance.

## VIII. Planning Infrastructure

### Pre-seizure

**PlanStore operational**
The federation PlanStore is live, the scope's data is in it, social recipients are registered, and the Observer procedure is running. On the day of seizure, the Observer's report is already current — workers can see exactly what they are managing.

**Desired Plan is ready**
The Desired Plan — the plan workers would implement if they managed — is already drafted, evaluated, and approved by the assembly. It includes:
- Autonomous plans ready to implement immediately
- Social-Dependent plans already requested from the federation (so the federation can activate them without delay)
- Infeasible plans documented (to prevent wasted effort)

The seizure should not be the moment workers first discuss what they want to produce. That discussion must have already happened, and its outcome must be recorded in the PlanStore as a binding plan.

**Two-plan transition visible**
Workers can see, in the PlanStore, the gap between what the capitalist's Current Plan was doing and what the Desired Plan will do. This visibility is motivational: the transition from Current to Desired is not abstract. Workers can see it in their own data.

## The Infrastructure Is the Transition

The readiness checklist above is not a list of things to do after workers decide to seize. It is the work of the entire period of Scope Committee operation — the mapping phase, the exposure phase, the control phase — accumulated over months or years before the management phase begins.

Every Observer report is a row in the scope map. Every recipe documented is a line in the Desired Plan. Every supplier contact made is a relationship pre-positioned. Every worker trained in the Observer procedure is one fewer single point of failure.

The committee's daily work is not preparation for revolution as an event. It is the construction, piece by piece, of the infrastructure that makes it possible to keep producing when the capitalist's infrastructure is no longer available — or is turned against the workers who built it.

# Market Seizure: The Gap Between Physical Control and Commercial Control

_Workers who seize a factory control the machines. They do not automatically control the order book, the bank accounts, the contracts, or the financial infrastructure through which the scope connects to markets. This gap is a primary vector of capitalist counter-power after physical seizure._

---

## 1. What Physical Seizure Does and Does Not Convey

**Conveys:**
- Access to machinery, tooling, raw material stocks on-site
- Worker knowledge, skill, and productive capacity
- Physical space

**Does not convey:**
- Sales contracts (held in the legal name of the company, not the factory)
- Purchase contracts with suppliers
- Bank accounts and payment systems (controlled by directors or holding company)
- Receivables — money already owed for goods already delivered
- Credit facilities, overdraft lines
- Tax registrations (VAT, employer contributions, export licenses)
- Regulatory permits (food safety, environmental, export)
- IP, recipes, technical specifications (may be held in systems the capitalist can remotely lock)
- ERP, CRM, and accounting system access (cloud-hosted; revocable remotely)

The factory is physical. The company is legal. Workers seize the former; the capitalist retains the latter — at least initially.

---

## 2. The Order Book Problem

The existing order book is a set of legal obligations between the company (as legal entity) and its customers. Workers who produce against that order book are producing for a legal counterparty they do not control.

Consequences:

- **Customers may refuse to pay workers' committee.** The legal obligation is to the company. Customers who pay the workers' committee may themselves face liability if the company later asserts the payment was invalid.
- **The capitalist can redirect incoming payments.** If the BMW payment arrives in the company bank account, the capitalist controls that account. Workers produced the goods; the holding company collects the revenue.
- **The capitalist can claim the output.** Goods shipped under the company's existing contracts are legally the company's goods until the customer takes title. This creates vulnerability in transit.
- **Negotiations upstream are invisible.** Large contracts are often signed at holding company level — the BMW relationship is with "Rheinmetall Holdings GmbH," not the plant. The plant manager who received the order is gone. Workers cannot see what discount commitments, rebate structures, or delivery penalties exist.

The order book is therefore both an asset (existing demand) and a liability (existing obligations and power asymmetries the workers have no visibility into).

---

## 3. Financial Infrastructure Requirements

From the moment of seizure, the scope needs:

**Immediate:**
- A new bank account in the name of a recognised legal entity (cooperative, commune, association) that can receive and disburse payments
- A mechanism to pay workers (cash initially, if payroll systems are locked)
- A mechanism to pay continuing input suppliers (electricity, materials in progress)
- An emergency liquidity source — federation cash bridge — since receivables are likely inaccessible

**Short-term:**
- Renegotiated or new supply contracts directly in the name of the new legal entity
- Customer agreements to redirect payment to new accounts — this requires customers to break with the old legal counterparty (creating their own legal risk)
- New VAT/tax registration
- Insurance re-establishment (the old policy likely lapses with change of effective control)

**Medium-term:**
- Credit relationships — extremely difficult; no credit history in the new entity's name, no collateral recognised by conventional lenders. Federation must substitute.
- Interoperability with banking infrastructure (SWIFT, SEPA, local payment rails). Workers' financial systems must be able to send and receive in the formats markets require.

The historical pattern (Argentina's recovered factories, Catalonia 1936): scopes initially operate in informal or semi-formal financial modes — cash wages, informal invoicing, barter with friendly suppliers — while the legal entity is constituted and financial relationships are rebuilt. This period is the most vulnerable.

---

## 4. Power Relations in the Financial Interface

Banks are not neutral infrastructure. They are institutions with ownership structures and state relationships that align them with capitalist legality.

- **Account freezing:** The existing bank can freeze the company account at the request of the legal owner or by court order, cutting off access to accumulated cash and incoming receivables.
- **Credit denial:** New workers' cooperatives cannot access conventional credit lines at the moment they most need liquidity. Capital strike at the financial layer compounds the physical seizure.
- **Currency and payment rail control:** Cross-border payments (critical for export-facing scopes like Scope B) pass through correspondent banking networks that the capitalist state can instruct to deny service — as demonstrated routinely against sanctioned entities.
- **The holding company's intercompany loans:** Many factories have internal debt to their holding companies — loans that become immediately callable upon change of control. The holding company can assert the workers' scope owes it money, creating a legal cloud over the scope's assets.

The capitalist does not need to retake the factory floor to strangle the scope. Cutting the bank account, rerouting the receivables, and calling the intercompany loan may be sufficient to induce collapse within weeks.

---

## 5. Sales Negotiated Outside the Scope

In large enterprises, commercial relationships are typically managed above plant level:

| Function | Where it sits | Workers' access |
| :------- | :------------ | :-------------- |
| Customer contracts | Holding company / HQ sales team | None initially |
| Supplier contracts | Procurement (often centralised) | None initially |
| Pricing decisions | Commercial leadership, above plant | None |
| Logistics / customs / export documentation | Centralised | Partial (if logistics staff are scope workers) |
| Financial instruments (letters of credit, export insurance) | Treasury / HQ | None |
| IT systems (ERP, order management) | IT / cloud provider | Revocable remotely |

Workers on the factory floor may have never met the customer's procurement officer. The relationship was managed by a sales director now loyal to the holding company, who can redirect the customer to an alternative supplier before workers have made a single call.

The scope's existing commercial position — years of relationship-building, qualification approvals, preferred supplier status — is not in the machines. It is in people and records the workers may not control.

---

## 6. What This Implies for Transition Strategy

The physical seizure is not the endpoint of transition strategy — it is the beginning of a race to constitute financial and commercial infrastructure before the capitalist can use financial power to reverse the seizure.

**Pre-positioning matters:** The committee should, where possible, establish relationships with customers and suppliers before seizure. If a customer already knows the committee and respects its legitimacy, redirecting payment is politically easier. If a supplier already has a relationship with the federation, continuity of supply is more reliable.

**Federation financial infrastructure is not optional:** The federation must be able to act as a bank of last resort for seized scopes — providing bridge liquidity, absorbing output, and covering input costs while commercial relationships are reconstituted in the scope's new legal name. This is not charity; it is the financial rear that makes the physical advance defensible.

**Legal entity formation must be prepared in advance:** Incorporating a cooperative or workers' association takes time. If done in a rush after seizure, under legal pressure from the capitalist, the process is compromised. Where possible, the legal entity should be constituted pre-emptively — the committee exists, the bylaws are drafted, the bank relationship is begun — so that the moment of physical seizure is also the moment the legal entity is ready to act.

**The Observer must track financial flows, not only physical ones:** After seizure, the capitalist's primary counter-move is financial. The Observer must monitor:
- Receivables that were issued pre-seizure: who is collecting them?
- Incoming payments: are they arriving in a controllable account?
- Supplier payment obligations: which are imminently due and what is the coverage?
- Intercompany loans: are they being called?

**Customer relationships are a political question:** Some customers (state transport authorities, public hospitals, school federations) can be approached politically — their procurement officers answer to political pressure that private customers do not. Export-facing scopes are more exposed, because foreign buyers have no political relationship with the workers and face no local accountability.

---

## 7. The Deeper Problem: Who Controls the Interface?

The financial infrastructure of capitalism — banking, payment rails, credit rating, corporate law — constitutes the interface between production and market. Workers can control production without controlling the interface. The interface remains in capitalist hands: the bank, the legal system, the correspondent network, the credit bureau.

This is not a technical problem. It is a power problem. The interface was designed to enforce capitalist property rights. Workers using it are using infrastructure built against them.

Two responses:

**Short-run:** Use the interface strategically and minimally. Establish alternative financial channels (federation treasury, mutual credit, barter with social recipients) to reduce dependence on the capitalist interface. Accept that some commercial relationships are lost — the brands that will not deal with a workers' cooperative are brands that were always one cancellation away from severing the relationship.

**Long-run:** Build a parallel financial infrastructure within the social plan that can eventually replace the capitalist interface for intra-federation transactions. When the majority of a scope's inputs come from social suppliers and its outputs go to social recipients, the dependency on capitalist banking falls to the residual market fraction. At `social_output_fraction = 0.90`, the scope needs conventional banking only for 10% of its commercial activity.

The social plan's financial infrastructure is not a secondary feature. It is the condition of possibility for sustaining physical seizures against financial counter-attack.

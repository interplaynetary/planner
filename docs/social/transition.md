# **Transitional Strategy**

# **Draft Specification**

**`Communization of Knowledge:`**
_`The goal is that no community, however isolated, should lack access to the sum total of human productive knowledge.`_

- **`Open Recipe Libraries:`** `Indexed by SNE, metabolic impact, and bioregional suitability.`
- **`Key Components:`** `OpenSourceEcologies, Documentation, Translation, and other key recipes.`

**`Transition in High-Income Regions:`**
_`High-income regions face a specific contradiction: they import most real goods but have idle labor and resources.`_

- **`Strategic Priority:`** `Organizing the unemployed and precariously employed to meet local needs through local production using open recipes.`
- **`Effect:`** `Reduces import dependence, builds alternative institutions, demonstrates planner capability, creates material pressure on wages in the remaining market sector, and prepares the ground for deeper transition when crisis hits.`

**`Transition in Low-Income Regions:`**

**`Scope Committees:`**
_`Scopes are spatial assemblages of economic resources (factories, farms, etc.). Scope Committees are elected by workers within a scope to steward the transition of production.`_

- **`Rights:`**
  - **`Decisions:`** `Internal production processes (subject to broader federation decisions).`
  - **`Immediately-Revoke:`** `Committee members, delegates, and technical consultants.`
  - **`Access & Communicate:`** `Full visibility into all internal operations, business secrets, and metabolic flows. Right to publish all findings.`
  - **`Inspect & Propose:`** `Send observers to trace metabolic connections; propose alternative plans.`
- **`Responsibilities:`**
  - **`To electors:`** `Faithful execution of binding mandates, regular reporting, economic training.`
  - **`To federation:`** `Provide accurate capacity data, abide by delegated decisions.`
  - **`To external-constituency:`** `Grant standing to materially affected non-workers.`
- **`Scope Hierarchy:`** `Workplace -> Enterprise -> Branch -> Regional -> National -> International.`
- **`Dual Power Asymmetry:`**
  _`Dual power is an unstable equilibrium where two authorities claim legitimacy: the capitalist's and the committee's. This cannot last. The committee either:`_
  1. `Accumulates power (through production, worker loyalty, militia strength) until it displaces the capitalist.`
  2. `Is co-opted (incorporated into management, separated from workers) and becomes a new layer of control.`
     _`If the committee's compensation diverges from workers', co-optation is underway.`_

**`Gradual Transition Within the Scope:`**
_`A scope cannot sever market connections overnight without metabolic collapse. Transition is sequential.`_

- **`Two Parallel Plans:`**
  - **`Current Plan (Capitalist):`** `Market-sourced inputs, market-sold outputs, hierarchical labor.`
  - **`Desired Plan (Social):`** `Socially-sourced inputs, socially-useful outputs, cooperative labor.`
- **`Gradual Substitution Ladder:`**
  - `(1) Observation -> (2) Partial Trial -> (3) Evaluation -> (4) Expansion -> (5) [CONFLICT] -> (6) Defense -> (7) Consolidation -> (8) Full Social Provision.`
  - _`The transition is not smooth. At the Conflict rung, the capitalist refuses, or the state intervenes, or scabs cross picket lines. The militia appears to defend the transition.`_

**`Scope Committee Observer Procedure:`**
_`The Observer measures the gap between Current and Desired plans. The Observer publishes all findings openly, making visible how much output goes to the social vs market and comparing expected compensation. The numbers serve as an organizing tool for assemblies and public meetings, not just a technical calculation.`_

- **`Physical Flow Fractions:`**
  - `x = Q_in_social / Q_in_total` _(fraction of inputs from social plan)_
  - `y = Q_out_social / Q_out_total` _(fraction of outputs to social plan)_
- **`Dollar Solvency Constraint:`**
  - `(R_market + R_social_dollar + D_received) ≥ (C_market + C_social_dollar + W_dollar + O_dollar + D_sent)`
- **`Labor Validation & Resistance:`**
  - `V_i = socially validated hours (judged by social plan recipients)`
  - `Expected Relationship:` `average(V_i) ≈ y × H_total_i`
  - _`Validation is a social judgment, not an automatic calculation. If the federation's validation systematically falls below the expected y proportion, workers will resist the transition.`_
- **`Compensation Equations:`**
  - **`Dollar Wage (from market revenue):`**
    `total_dollar_wages = (1-y) × (total_output_value_in_dollars) × (labor_share_of_revenue)`
    `dollar_wage_i = total_dollar_wages × (H_total_i / H_total) × some_equity_factor`
    _`If y increases but dollar wages do not decrease proportionally (because workers resist wage cuts), the factory becomes insolvent.`_
  - **`Claim Capacity (from social pool):`**
    `claim_capacity_i = V_i × (available_claimable_pool / total_social_svc) + solidarity_supplement_i`
- **`The substitution ratio (Real Purchasing Power):`**
  - `fairness_ratio = [claim_purchasing_power × y] / [dollar_purchasing_power × (1-y)]`
  - _`Workers compare purchasing power, not nominal wages. If ratio ≈ 1, compensation is accepted. claim_purchasing_power equals what 1 unit of capacity buys in the local claim pool; dollar_purchasing_power equals what $1 buys in local markets.`_
  - _`Political usage:`_ `The substitution ratio is published for every scope. Workers can see whether their social compensation matches their market compensation, whether the gap is growing or shrinking with y, and how their scope compares to others. Transparency turns compensation into a democratic question: "Why is our substitution ratio 0.7 when the neighboring scope's is 1.1?" Assemblies debate, federations adjust, and the Observer recalculates. This makes the ratio an organizing tool, not just a metric for the committee.`
- **`Federation Role in Transition Fairness:`**
  - `For proposed (x_next, y_next), the Observer computes required D_received (subsidy) to balance dollars.`
  - `If fairness_ratio_next << 1 or D_received exceeds capacity, the federation must:`
    1. `Increase available claimable pool (reclassify goods to individual-claimable)`
    2. `Validate more hours (if currently under-validating)`
    3. `Provide dollar subsidies (compensating wage loss)`
    4. `Negotiate a slower y increase.`
  - _`Note: Dollar subsidies without concurrently expanding the social claim pool creates dependency, not transition.`_

**`Global Claim Pool & Political Sustainability:`**
_`One hour of socially-validated labor anywhere yields the same baseline claim capacity. The claim pool makes visible what capitalist prices hide: that an hour producing food in Mozambique and an hour producing financial instruments in Luxembourg are not inherently unequal. Their apparent inequality under capitalism reflects not real value but the power to extract.`_

_`The global pool inverts this: value is determined by social validation of useful output.`_

_`Whether this massive reorientation is politically sustainable depends on material interdependence:`_

- `Do high-income regions produce anything low-income regions actually need?`
- `If yes, the relationship is mutual—each needs what the other produces.`
- `If no, their claim on the global pool rests on convincing others that their labor deserves equal recognition despite producing little of use.`
- _`The transition's political sustainability rests on this material truth, not on moral appeals. Regions that produce real goods hold material power. Regions that produce only claims on future production hold only persuasion.`_

**`Social Plan Defense:`**
_`The workers' militia is the armed extension of the picket line, defending the conditions of possibility for planning.`_

- **`Formation:`** `Self-defense groups drawn from workplaces and neighborhoods; coordinated regionally.`
- **`Command Structure:`** `Immediate revocability; bound by imperative mandate of the defending community.`
- **`Character of Defense:`** `Defensive, proportional, accountable, temporary. Replaces the standing bourgeois army until organized armed force itself becomes obsolete.`
- **`Purpose:`** `Protect PlanStores, assemblies, and transition committees from violent disruption or expropriated sabotage.`
